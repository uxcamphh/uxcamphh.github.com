Was und Wann

Das Erste

Am 18. August fand das erste UX Camp in Hamburg statt –ein Tag voller spannender Vorträge, Diskussionen und Inspirationen. Unser sommerliches Camp fand in den Räumlichkeiten unseres Location-Sponsors Sinnerschrader statt, wo wir über gut ausgestattete Konfis hinaus auch schattige Arkadengänge und eine schöne grüne Wiese für Sessions anbieten konnten.

Keinen Plan was ein BarCamp überhaupt ist?

Wikipedia gibt Auskunft:

Ein Barcamp (häufig auch: BarCamp, Unkonferenz, Ad-hoc-Nicht-Konferenz) ist eine offene Tagung mit offenen Workshops, deren Inhalte und Ablauf von den Teilnehmern zu Beginn der Tagung selbst entwickelt und im weiteren Verlauf gestaltet werden.

Nach dem Camp ist vor dem Camp

Es hat euch Spaß gemacht, es hat uns Spaß gemacht, warum also keine weitere Iteration des UX Camp Hamburgs? Abonniert das Newsletter, folgt uns auf Twitter @uxcamphh oder geht Bier mit uns trinken. Dann seid ihr die ersten, die von Neuigkeiten erfahrt. Und dran denken, weitersagen ist gut fürs Karma!

How do a get a ticket?

We want to foster the idea that there no spectators at a barcamp, just participants. So the safest way to attend the camp is to get involved:

  1. Become a helping hand. You either help us prepare things on friday, or help out a couple hours on saturday. We will try so you can see as much sessions as possible.
  2. Bring a session.
  3. Need to plan travel and accomodation? Drop us a line! We consider getting on the road a form of participation :)
  4. Join the "raffle". The rest of the tickets will be given away on a first-come-first-serve basis. We will announce date and time on the mailing list. It is then yours to set an alarm and keep hitting the refresh button. Last year all tickets where gone within three hours.

Sessions

Bring your session, get a ticket!

The more sessions we habe the camp, the better. So we want top favor people who bring a session. That does not mean that you have to prepare a frontal speech. A session can also be an open discussion, exchange of expreiences, failures or best practices – or a workshop.

So, here is the deal: post your session title and a quick outline to your blog and send us the link. We will put that in the list below and you will get a ticket token in return. (The rest of the tickets will be available on a first-come-first-server basis, date and time will be send via the mailing list.)

The low down

The first UX Camp in Hamburg took place on 18 August – for people who want to create better products and services. It was a full day of exciting talks, discussions and inspirations. And delicious food, of course. Seeing it took place in summer, we arranged for an awesome location at Sinnerschrader that not only provided superior conference rooms but relaxing green outside areas. Talks were held in German and English language.

But don't take our word for it. Check out reviews and pictures. And spread the word! (#uxcamphh)

Stay up to date with our newsletter or follow us on the Twitters @uxcamphh to be part of this exciting event next year as well.
