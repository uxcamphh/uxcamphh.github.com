About UX Camp Hamburg

The Second

The second UX Camp Hamburg is about to happen on August 17, 2013. It is a BarCamp on the topic of User Experience. The independently organized event will take place at SinnerSchrader, our beloved location partner. It is by all means a summer camp: besides well-equipped conference rooms on the inside, the location also features roofed and open-air spaces for outside sessions. There will be lunch, there will be BBQ, there will be cold drinks. There will definitely be an after-party (details will follow) and we are working on a warm-up on friday evening.  What you need to provide: cool sessions!  If you are wondering now what that means, or what a Barcamp is anyways, read "What is a BarCamp?".

TICKETS

How do I a get a ticket?

First of all: tickets are free of charge. The whole event is financed by our beloved sponsors. But we want to foster the idea that there are "no spectators at a barcamp, only participants". So here are your three options to join the camp:

  1. Become a helping hand. You either help us prepare things on friday, or help out a couple hours on saturday. We will try so you can see as much sessions as possible. You will probably carry around stuff, set stuff up, and clean the stuff.
  2.  Bring a session. 
  3. The rest of the tickets will be given away on a first-come-first-serve basis. We will announce date and time on the mailing list. It is then yours to set an alarm and keep hitting the refresh button. Last year all tickets where gone within three hours.

Coming from abroad? Need to plan travel and accomodation? Drop us a line!

Sessions

Bring your session, get an early ticket!

We want to favor people who bring a session.  That does not mean that you have to prepare slides or a frontal speech.  A session can also be an open discussion, exchange of experiences/failures/best practices, getting feedback on your app – or a workshop, of course. For inspiration check out the schedule of UX Camp Europe.

So, here is the deal: post your session title and a quick outline to your blog and send us the link. We will put that in a list here on the site and you will get a ticket token in return. (The rest of the tickets will be available on a first-come-first-serve basis, date and time will be send via the mailing list.)

Anouncing and scheduling will happen at normal session planning in the morning.
